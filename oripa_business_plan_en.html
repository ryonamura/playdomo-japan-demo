2. Return Rate: Industry Reality and DOMO's Strategy

2-1. The “99% Return Rate” Trick

  • Formula: Total market value of included items / (Price per pull × Total pulls) × 100
  • “Market value” is calculated based on retail price (1.5–2× the buyback price)
  • Actual return rates are around 87% for DOPA! and 91% for Pokekuro
  • Procurement cost is 60–70% of market price → even “99% return” yields a 30–40% gross margin

2-2. DOMO's Strategy: Honest Return Rates × Cosmos Economy

While many competitors inflate their “headline return rate” by assigning inflated values to cards, DOMO differentiates with honest return rates based on real product market prices.

Existing Oripa (Predatory) Existing Oripa (Reputable) DOMO
Displayed Return Rate 200–500% (inflated) 85–99% (market price basis) 70–85% (honest display)
Losing Tier Padded with cards of dubious value Low-value trading cards DOMO goods + bonus card (Cosmos reward)
Differentiator None (erodes trust) Product selection, shipping speed Cosmos economy, sweepstakes Draw, experiential prizes
DOMO's winning formula: Rather than competing on return rate numbers, attract users with the ecosystem's appeal — “Every oripa pull earns Cosmos, and the Draw (sweepstakes) might win you an experiential prize.”

3. DOMO Card — Designed as a Cosmos Earning Medium

DOMO cards do not claim any market value as oripa prizes. They are included strictly as a “bonus,” and their function is as a Cosmos (point) earning medium.

Key Design Principle: Assigning a fictitious market value to DOMO cards to inflate the return rate poses legal risk under the Act against Unjustifiable Premiums and Misleading Representations, and damages user trust. The card's value is limited to its functional value of “Cosmos rewards.”

3-3. Collection Incentive Mechanics

  1. Completion Rewards — Bonus Cosmos (50–200) for completing themed sets

  4. Story Cards — QR codes unlock DOMO universe storylines
  5. Holographic / Foil Finishes — Higher rarities differentiated by physical texture

9. Risks and Countermeasures

Iranoan Patent (No. 7472179)

  • Scope: Online trading card lottery sales system
  • Impact on DOMO: Medium-Low (DOMO primarily uses non-trading-card products; “physical goods sales + card bonus” differs structurally)
  • Countermeasure: Commission patent attorney for investigation in Phase 0, design around the patent during development, join licensing program if needed

Competitive Differentiation

Competitor Strength DOMO's Differentiator
DOPA! High return rate, 60 animation types Non-trading-card products, Cosmos / Draw experience, native app
Japan Trading Card Center No.1 revenue, 24h shipping Appeal of real products, access to experiential prizes
clove Design quality, UI Cosmos economy, 3-layer ecosystem

Premiums and Representations Act Risk

  • Since no fictitious market value is assigned to DOMO cards, risk of misleading representation is low
  • Return rate is honestly calculated based on real product market prices
  • Sweepstakes Draw is designed as open sweepstakes, so no prize value cap applies
  • Countermeasure: Proactively implement self-regulation on probability disclosure and ceiling / pity system; comply with Consumer Affairs Agency guidelines

Other Risks

Risk Countermeasure
Inventory risk (Apple new product launches depreciate older models) S Prize ordered after purchase; inventory turnover within 2 weeks
Unappealing goods (losing tier dissatisfaction) Invest in design quality, seasonal refresh, incorporate user feedback

Minor protection Restrict access for users under 18; monthly spending cap of ¥10,000
