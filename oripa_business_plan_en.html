1. Executive Summary

This business plan outlines how DOMO can enter Japan's online oripa (random pack) market with a proprietary high-return-rate model that leverages DOMO original cards to deliver surface return rates of 100–200% while preserving gross margins of 20–40%.

Core Thesis: DOMO original cards (production cost ¥30–80 per card) are assigned "collection value" through rarity tiers, limited editions, and community-driven demand. This allows surface return rates of 100–200% on every pack while maintaining healthy operator margins — a model that is structurally impossible for competitors who rely on third-party TCGs with fixed wholesale costs.

Key Highlights

  • Unique positioning: 95%+ of competitors are TCG-focused. DOMO takes a unique position with non-TCG × proprietary cards, a combination with no direct competitor
  • Return rate advantage: Surface return rates of 100–200% (vs. industry standard 90–99%), with actual gross margins of 20–40%
  • 12-month total budget: ¥68M–110M across four phases
  • Breakeven point: Approximately 1,200 paying users (at ¥3,000 ARPU)
  • Research date: February 26, 2026

2. Industry Return Rate Reality & DOMO's Strategy

How the "99% Return Rate" Actually Works

The oripa industry widely advertises return rates of 90–99%, but this figure is calculated on retail/market price, not procurement cost. Understanding this gap is the key to the entire business model.

Metric Industry Standard DOMO Model
Advertised return rate 90–99% 100–200%
Basis for return rate Market retail price Assigned collection value
Actual procurement cost 60–70% of market price ¥30–80 per card
Operator gross margin 30–40% 20–40% (competitive, but sustainable)

The "99% Return Rate" Trick Explained

  • Return rate is calculated as: (total value of items in pack at retail price) ÷ (pack purchase price)
  • A card with a market price of ¥10,000 may have been procured at ¥6,000–7,000 (wholesale or buyback at 60–70%)
  • This 1.5–2× markup between buyback price and retail price is where the margin lives
  • Competitors are structurally capped at ~99% stated return rate because third-party TCG procurement costs are fixed

DOMO's "Cheat Weapon": Original Cards

DOMO original cards cost ¥30–80 to produce but can be assigned a surface collection value of ¥300–5,000+ through rarity design, limited editions, and community-driven demand. This 4–160× markup is the engine that powers return rates competitors cannot match.

7 Value-Building Mechanisms

  1. Completion Rewards
    Collecting a full set of a series (e.g., all 12 "Zodiac" cards) unlocks exclusive in-app rewards, Cosmos bonuses, and a physical completion certificate. This drives repeat purchases and trading.
  2. Serial Numbers
    All Legendaire and above cards are individually serial numbered (e.g., #007/500). Low serial numbers (#001–#010) command premium secondary market prices, as proven in the TCG and sneaker industries.
  3. Hologram & Premium Printing
    Multi-layer holographic foil, embossed textures, and premium card stock create tangible perceived value that justifies the assigned price above production cost.
  4. Artist Collaborations
    Partnerships with Japanese illustrators, manga artists, and digital creators produce limited-run cards with genuine artistic merit and built-in fan audiences.
  5. Seasonal & Event Limited Editions
    Cards tied to specific dates or events (New Year, DOMO anniversary, Tokyo Game Show) are never reprinted, creating genuine scarcity and FOMO-driven demand.
  6. Story Cards (Lore Series)
    Cards that form a narrative arc across multiple series, encouraging collectors to pursue complete story sequences. Each card reveals part of the DOMO universe lore.
  7. Card "Awakening" (Evolution System)
    Specific card combinations can be "awakened" into upgraded versions via in-app mechanics. The awakened card has a new design and higher collection tier, consuming the input cards and reducing supply.

Monetization Funnel

The gacha UI/UX is designed to move users through a clear spending progression:

  1. Free: Download app, browse collection, view other users' pulls
  2. Registration Bonus: 1 free pull on signup → taste the opening animation experience
  3. Login Habit: Daily login → free Cosmos → return daily to accumulate
  4. Small Purchase: ¥100–300 packs → low barrier first transaction
  5. Rank Up: Unlock pack tiers and collection milestones → invest in progression
  6. Mid Purchase: ¥500–1,000 packs → chase specific rarities
  7. Ceiling Chase: Pity system guarantees motivate "just a few more" draws
  8. High Purchase: ¥3,000–5,000 packs → chase Genesis/Artist editions

Breakeven Analysis

Breakeven point: approximately 1,200 paying users at ¥3,000 ARPU with ¥3.5M monthly fixed operating costs. This is achievable within Phase 1 (Month 1–3) under Standard and Optimistic scenarios.
  • Monthly fixed costs: ~¥3.5M (personnel, server, office)
  • Contribution margin per user: ~¥2,900/month (at ¥3,000 ARPU after variable costs)
  • Breakeven: ¥3.5M ÷ ¥2,900 = ~1,207 users

8. Cosmos Economy (Tier 2)

Cosmos is DOMO's in-app currency that creates a secondary engagement and monetization layer beyond direct oripa purchases.

Cosmos Earning Methods

9. Risks & Mitigation

Iranoan Patent (No. 7472179)

Item Assessment
Impact level Medium-Low
Scope Broadly covers online random pack sales mechanisms. Multiple industry players potentially infringe but no enforcement actions observed to date.
Mitigation Patent attorney review in Phase 0 (budgeted). Design around claims if needed. DOMO's original card model may fall outside the patent's TCG-focused claims.
Worst case Licensing fee negotiation (~1–3% of revenue). Not a business-ending risk.

Competition Differentiation

Competitor Type Example DOMO Differentiation
TCG-focused oripa DOPA!, Japan TCC, clove Non-TCG positioning, original cards, Cosmos economy. No direct competition.
Non-TCG gacha KURAND, HafH, Ichiban Kuji Card collecting as core mechanic (not just physical prizes). App-native vs. web-based.
DMM (D-Oripa) D-Oripa DOMO has original IP and French brand story vs. DMM's platform approach. Different target audience.
SaaS entrants (JoyBase) Small operators DOMO has proprietary card ecosystem, premium UX, and marketing budget that SaaS-based entrants cannot match.

Card Value Establishment Risk (Highest Risk)

This is the single biggest risk in the entire business plan. DOMO original cards have zero established market value at launch. If users do not perceive them as collectible, the entire return-rate model collapses.
Factor Mitigation Strategy
No existing collector community Seed community via influencer partnerships (YouTubers opening DOMO packs); pre-launch Discord with early access
No secondary market history Enable in-app trading in Phase 2; partner with Mercari/Yahoo Auctions for visibility; "buy one, list one" campaign
Perceived as "worthless paper" High production quality (holographic, serial numbers); artist collaborations with recognizable names; completion rewards with tangible value
Slow initial adoption Launch packs at 200%+ return rate (subsidized); Genesis Edition scarcity creates urgency; physical prize inclusion ensures minimum perceived value

Additional Risks

Risk Level Mitigation
Inventory risk (physical prizes) Medium Start with digital-only packs; add physical prizes in Phase 2–3 with pre-order model; use point-conversion to manage unsold inventory
Regulatory risk (gambling classification) Medium Structure as purchase contract (goods always delivered); full probability disclosure; attorney opinion obtained in Phase 0
Minor protection (underage users) High importance Age verification at signup; monthly spending caps for users under 20 (¥5,000/month); parental consent flow; comply with JOGA guidelines
App store rejection Medium Full probability disclosure meets Apple/Google requirements; structure avoids "loot box" classification by always guaranteeing items of value
Card counterfeiting Low QR code verification on every card; holographic security features; NFC chip in Legendaire+ cards; blockchain-backed certificate of authenticity
