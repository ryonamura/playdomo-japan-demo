Section 1: Legal Nature of DOMO Cards (Most Critical)

Conclusion: PlayDOMO Is a "Lottery-Based Digital Collectible with Prizes"

PlayDOMO is not NFT/blockchain-based. There is no secondary market for cards.

How It Fundamentally Works

  1. Purchase a card pack (financial sacrifice)
  2. Rarity determined by chance (gacha element)
  3. Accumulation of Cosmos tokens (1 card = 1 Cosmos)
  4. Eligibility to enter a lottery for high-value physical prizes (e.g., Lamborghini)

Technical Specifications of Cards

  • No NFT / blockchain technology
  • Cosmos tokens: tied to individual account, non-transferable, no expiration
  • No trading on external marketplaces
  • Free participation: 1 free pack opened per day + up to 10 tokens obtainable via postal mail

  • Rolex Batman (2–3 million JPY / ~$13–20K USD)

Self-Classification under French Law

"jeux promotionnels par tirage au sort" (promotional games by lottery)

  • The official site explicitly states "Place your bet" (French: "misez")
  • Geographic scope: Metropolitan France only
  • Age restriction: 18 years and over

Applicability of the JONUM Regulation

Conclusion: Not subject to JONUM

Of the four JONUM requirements, the fourth (ONUM = monetizable digital object) is not satisfied.

  • Cosmos tokens are tied to individual accounts and non-transferable → do not qualify as ONUMs
  • PlayDOMO itself also avoids any reference to the JONUM regulation

Legal Nature of the Cosmos Token

Applicability as Prepaid Payment Instrument (前払式支払手段): High likelihood

Requirement Assessment Rationale
Issued for consideration Applicable Issued in exchange for card purchase payment
Stores value Applicable Recorded as 1 Cosmos = value of 1 card
Exercisable right Applicable Can be used to unlock booster packs

No expiration → does not fall under the 6-month exception → notification obligation triggers when unused balance exceeds 10 million JPY (~$67K USD)

Applicability as Crypto Asset: Not applicable

  • Cannot be transferred or assigned to third parties
  • No function to exchange for fiat currency
  • No blockchain infrastructure

Risk Analysis under Japanese Law (Critically Important)

Lottery Crime — Criminal Code Article 187 (富くじ罪・刑法187条): Extremely High Risk

All four requirements are met:

  • Acquisition or loss of property (Lamborghini, etc.)
  • Element of chance (card rarity, lottery draw)
  • Financial sacrifice (pack purchase fee)
  • Competition for gain among multiple participants

Gambling Offense — Criminal Code Article 185 (賭博罪・刑法185条): High Risk

Likely to be interpreted as a wager targeting high-value prizes such as the Lamborghini (500–600 million JPY / ~$3.3–4M USD). The official use of the phrase "Place your bet" could serve as direct evidence.

Act against Unjustifiable Premiums and Misleading Representations (景品表示法): Certain Violation

The prize value far exceeds the legal cap (20 times the transaction amount or 100,000 JPY / ~$670 USD, whichever is lower).

Payment Services Act (資金決済法): Possible notification obligation

If Cosmos tokens qualify as prepaid payment instruments, obligations arise to deposit a security deposit and file a notification with authorities.

Important Note: The prevailing view in Japan is that the "free alternative entry route" (postal mail participation) adopted in France does not eliminate illegality under Japanese law.
