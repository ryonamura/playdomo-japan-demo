1. Executive Summary

This report identifies the optimal product lineup for DOMO's entry into Japan's oripa (online random pack) market. The research covers the current competitive landscape, popular genres, business model economics, and legal considerations.

Key Findings

  • Market opportunity: Japan's TCG market reached ¥302.4B in FY2024, with online oripa estimated in the hundreds of billions of yen
  • Competitive landscape: 50+ services exist, with 95%+ focused exclusively on TCGs
  • Recommendation: DOMO should adopt a non-TCG × DOMO original card hybrid model to differentiate from existing players
  • Top product picks: Apple products, limited-edition sneakers, game consoles, figures, and DOMO original physical cards
  • Legal advantage: New products require no secondhand dealer license, reducing regulatory burden

6. Oripa Business Model

Revenue Structure

  • Stated return rate: 90-99% (calculated on market price basis)
  • Actual procurement cost: 60-70% of market price
  • Operator gross margin: Estimated 30-40%

Procurement Methods

  • Card shop wholesale: Procurement at 50-70% of market price
  • Bulk BOX purchase: Buy sealed boxes and open for singles
  • Self-buyback: Repurchase from users at 60-80% of market price for reuse

Industry SaaS

JoyBase: A zero-upfront-cost oripa site builder SaaS launched in 2026. Enables anyone to start an oripa business without development costs, further lowering barriers to entry.

10. Legal Considerations

Key Points

  • New products: No secondhand dealer license (kobutsu-eigyo kyoka) is required when selling new items
  • Iranoan patent (No. 7472179): Broadly covers online oripa mechanisms — attorney review is essential before launch
  • Premiums Act (Keihin Hyoji Ho): All items in a pack must have economic value; "blank" prizes (worthless items) are not permitted
  • Probability disclosure: Follow JOGA (Japan Online Game Association) and CESA (Computer Entertainment Supplier's Association) guidelines for full transparency
