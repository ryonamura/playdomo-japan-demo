1. Legal Framework Analysis of Japan's Oripa Market

Market Overview

  • The overall TCG market is approximately 250 billion JPY (~$1.67B USD) (2025). The oripa segment is estimated at several tens of billions to 100 billion JPY (~$670M USD)
  • DOPA! (largest in the industry, by sinsa Inc.), Iris (by crafty Inc., now a subsidiary of Accelmark), and clove (by TrustHub Inc.) are the major players
  • In February 2026, "D-Oripa" under the DMM group also entered the market

Core Legal Framework

Grounds for Not Being Classified as Gambling

  • Structured as a "purchase contract where goods (cards) are always received," with no contest over gain or loss of property
  • No "chance of winning or losing" for the operator either (total sales are fixed once all packs are sold)
  • "Public value" of trading cards does not exist (market price is not considered public value)

Attorney Opinions: Still a Gray Zone

However, attorney opinions still classify this as a gray zone. The following risks exist.
  • Risk that a point-conversion-to-re-gacha loop may be regarded as substantive "gain or loss of money"
  • Possibility that high-value cards may not qualify for the "items provided for momentary entertainment" exception
  • The October 2024 amendment to the Act against Unjustifiable Premiums and Misleading Representations (景品表示法) introduced criminal penalties for falsifying probabilities
  • Industry-wide guidelines remain unestablished (JOGA's gacha guidelines serve as a reference precedent)

Applicable Laws

Law Application
Secondhand Goods Business Act (古物営業法) License required when sourcing used cards
Act on Specified Commercial Transactions (特定商取引法) Regulates mail-order / distance selling
Act against Unjustifiable Premiums and Misleading Representations (景品表示法) False probability disclosures and misleading return rates are violations. Criminal penalties added in October 2024 amendment
Gambling offenses — Criminal Code Article 185 (賭博罪・刑法185条) Formally avoided by structuring as a purchase contract

Implications for DOMO

  • The structure of "always providing a product via a purchase contract" is the most critical element
  • Additional review needed on whether digital items qualify as "goods" (物品)
  • Design must avoid triggering complete gacha regulations (prohibition on card-combining mechanics)
  • Need to check applicability of the Payment Services Act (資金決済法) (prepaid payment instruments)
  • Full disclosure of probabilities is effectively mandatory
  • Avoid making point redemption too easily convertible to cash

3. Details of France's JONUM Regulation

Legal Framework

Item Details
Governing Law Loi SREN (No. 2024-449, May 21, 2024), Articles 40 and 41
Implementing Decree Décret No. 2026-60 (published February 4, 2026; effective February 7, 2026)
Nature 3-year experimental framework (May 2024 – May 2027). Operational period is approximately 15 months
Nickname "Loi Sorare" (Sorare was envisioned as a primary subject of application)

Four Cumulative Requirements (All Must Be Met to Qualify as JONUM)

  1. Financial sacrifice (sacrifice financier)
  2. Mechanism involving chance (mécanisme faisant appel au hasard)
  3. Online public communication service (service de communication au public en ligne)
  4. Acquisition of a monetizable digital object (ONUM)

Key Regulatory Requirements

  • Prior registration with ANJ (Autorité Nationale des Jeux) required (EU/EEA-headquartered companies only)
  • Participation by minors is strictly prohibited (mandatory age and identity verification)
  • Players must be able to self-set 7-day spending limits and gaming time limits
  • Self-exclusion devices (24 hours to 12 months) must be implemented
  • Prize caps: physical goods 1,000 EUR/person/game per year; cryptocurrency 25,000 EUR/person per year (stablecoins not permitted)
  • Total prize distribution must not exceed 20% of annual game revenue
  • AML/CFT obligations (KYC, TRACFIN reporting, blockchain transaction tracking)
  • Data retention: account data 5 years, certain assets 8 years
  • Penalties for violations: up to 3 years imprisonment + 90,000 EUR fine; administrative fines up to 5% of turnover

Permitted Game Types / Prohibited Mechanics

Status Details
Permitted Action and combat games, breeding games, fantasy sports and horse racing games
Prohibited Casino games (roulette, slot machines, etc.), traditional horse race betting

Comparison Across EU Countries

Country Approach Key Features
France Regulated authorization Created a new category (JONUM). Promotes innovation while managing risk
Belgium Full ban Ruled paid loot boxes as gambling. Fines up to 800,000 EUR
Netherlands Regulated under existing law Attempted to apply gambling laws but faced difficulties in court

JONUM Applicability Analysis for PlayDOMO

Scenario JONUM Assessment
Cards are NFT/blockchain-based and tradeable on secondary markets Likely in scope
Cards exist only as in-game items with no external trading Out of scope
Indirect monetization via in-game currency Gray zone (subject to ANJ interpretation)
French company — headquarters location requirement Satisfied

5. Overseas Case Studies of Experience-Based Gacha / Lotteries

Major Case Studies

Service Country Model Legal Structure Outcome
Omaze US → UK Charity + celebrity experience sweepstakes US: sweepstakes law CA investigated as "illegal lottery" → withdrew from US market
Prizeo / Charitybuzz US Celebrity experience sweepstakes / auction Sweepstakes law Cumulative $50M+ raised
DAYMADE UK Subscription-based lottery (£1.50/week, 1-in-3 win rate) Gambling Act 2005 free draw Successful. Mix of experiences and physical prizes
Wowcher Mystery Holiday UK Mystery travel (no losers) Product sale Successful. Everyone receives an experience
Pack Up + Go / Jubel US Customized surprise travel Product sale Successful. No lottery element
Tinggly Global Experience gift boxes (choice-based) Product sale Available in 100+ countries

Related Domestic Services in Japan

  • Ichiban Kuji (Bandai Spirits): Physical prizes only. Not experience-based
  • SOW EXPERIENCE: 180+ experience gifts. Choice-based (no lottery element)
  • asoview! Gift: 1,000+ experience partners. Choice-based

Key Finding

The triple structure of "TCG + gacha + experience lottery" has no known global precedent.
  • Lesson from Omaze: celebrity experiences alone do not scale — they pivoted to physical goods, then withdrew from the US market
  • DAYMADE's success pattern: low price + high win rate + experience-centric
  • A "no-loser" design is the strongest approach from both a legal risk and user satisfaction standpoint

Legal Framework Comparison by Country

Country Framework Avoidance Strategy
United States Sweepstakes law (Prize + Chance + Consideration = illegal) No Purchase Necessary (free entry option)
United Kingdom Gambling Act 2005 Free draw (provide a free entry route)
Japan Gambling offense (Criminal Code Article 185 — chance event + gain/loss of property) Structure as "purchase contract for goods"

Five Key Lessons DOMO Should Learn From

  1. Omaze's lesson: Celebrity experiences alone do not scale
  2. DAYMADE's success: Low-cost subscription + high win rate + experience-centric model
  3. Wowcher's lesson: "No-loser" design is the strongest
  4. Omaze's US exit: Legal compliance must be the top priority
  5. No global precedent: A gacha/lottery service with experiences as prizes has virtually no precedent worldwide
